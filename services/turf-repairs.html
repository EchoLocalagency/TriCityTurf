Turf Repair Services

Expert synthetic turf repairs for any brand or installer. We fix it right so your turf looks and performs like new.

Restore Your Turf

Professional Turf Repair Services in Vista and North County San Diego

Synthetic turf is built to last, but it is not indestructible. Seams can separate over time, reflected sunlight from windows can melt fibers, heavy furniture can crush pile, and poor original installation can lead to drainage problems. When your turf needs work, you need a team that knows exactly how to fix it. At Tri-City Synthetic Turf, we repair all types of synthetic turf damage, regardless of who installed it originally.

Seam separation is one of the most common issues we see. When seams pull apart, they create visible lines, tripping hazards, and entry points for weeds. Our repair process involves re-cutting the edges, applying fresh seam tape and adhesive, and securing the turf so the repair is invisible. We match the seam technique to the turf type and ensure the fix holds up under foot traffic and weather.

Burn damage from reflected sunlight is a growing problem in Southern California. Low-E windows on neighboring homes can focus sunlight like a magnifying glass, melting turf fibers in concentrated spots. We cut out the damaged section, patch in matching turf, and blend the repair into the surrounding area. If you are dealing with recurring burn damage, we can also advise on reflective film solutions to address the root cause.

Infill replenishment is another service homeowners often overlook. Over the years, infill material gets displaced by rain, pets, foot traffic, and leaf blowers. When infill levels drop, the turf loses its support structure. Blades lay flat instead of standing upright, drainage slows down, and the surface feels different underfoot. We assess your infill levels and add the right type and amount to restore proper performance.

Drainage issues typically trace back to the original installation. If the base was not graded or compacted correctly, water pools on the surface instead of draining through. We can often correct drainage problems without removing the entire turf by addressing specific low spots or adding drainage channels underneath.
